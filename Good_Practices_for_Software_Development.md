# Good Practices for Software Development  
---
## Question 1: Which point(s) were new to you?

The concept of over-communicating in a professional way was new to me.  
I understood that it is important to immediately inform the team if there are changes in requirements, delays, or technical issues instead of waiting until the last moment.

The idea of asking better questions was also very useful. Instead of simply saying "It is not working," we should clearly explain the issue, mention what we have already tried, and provide screenshots or code snippets. This makes it easier for others to help.

Learning about deep work and protecting focus by reducin g distractions such as social media was also an important takeaway.

---

## Question 2: Which area do you think you need to improve on? What are your ideas to make progress in that area?

I think I need to improve in the area of communication, especially in clearly explaining problems and giving regular updates to the team.

To improve in this area, I plan to:
- Write down clear notes during requirement discussions.
- Share updates with the team regularly instead of waiting.
- Practice explaining issues in a structured way (problem, what I tried, expected result, actual result).
- Use screenshots or code snippets while asking technical questions.
- Join meetings early and participate more actively.

I also want to improve my focus during work hours.  
For this, I will:
- Keep my phone on silent while working.
- Reduce social media usage during work time.
- Track my time to understand how I spend it.
- Maintain a healthy routine with proper food and exercise.

By working consistently on these areas, I believe I can become a more professional and productive software developer.
