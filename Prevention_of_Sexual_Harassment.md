# Prevention of Sexual Harassment (PoSH)

## 1) What kinds of behaviour cause sexual harassment?

Sexual harassment means any unwanted behaviour of a sexual nature that makes a person feel uncomfortable, scared, embarrassed, or unsafe.

It can include:

### Physical Behaviour
- Unwanted touching, hugging, or brushing against someone  
- Blocking someone’s way  
- Asking for physical closeness when the other person is not interested  

### Verbal Behaviour
- Making sexual jokes or comments  
- Commenting on someone’s body or appearance in a sexual way  
- Asking for sexual favours  
- Repeatedly asking someone out even after they say no  

### Non-Verbal Behaviour
- Staring in an uncomfortable way  
- Making sexual gestures  
- Sending inappropriate messages, photos, or emojis  
- Showing sexual pictures or videos  

## 2) What would you do if you face or witness such behaviour?

### If you face harassment:
- Clearly tell the person to stop (if you feel safe doing so).  
- Stay away from the person if possible.  
- Save proof like messages, emails, screenshots, etc.  
- Report the incident to HR or the Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) in your organization.  
> **Important:**  
> If the behaviour is unwelcome and makes someone uncomfortable, it can be considered sexual harassment.

---
### If you witness harassment:
- Support the person who is affected.  
- Ask them if they are okay.  
- Encourage them to report it.  
- Report the incident yourself if needed.  

Ignoring the situation can allow it to continue. Speaking up helps create a safe workplace.
